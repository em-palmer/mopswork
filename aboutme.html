Project 1

AI-Driven Lead Data Normalisation & Deduplication Architecture · Company 1

Situation
The CRM and Marketo databases—housing over 2,000,000 records—were severely degraded by data fragmentation, malformed entries, and high duplication rates. This unstructured data diluted campaign segmentation accuracy, inflated email bounce rates, compromised lead scoring logic, and forced the marketing team to waste hours each month on manual data cleansing.
Task
My objective was to architect and execute a company-wide, AI-driven data normalisation and deduplication project. The framework needed to parse a massive dataset, resolve complex merging conflicts (such as differentiating Partner versus End User account structures and navigating locked Support Contacts), and establish a sustainable, automated monthly data hygiene cadence.
Action
  • AI Integration & Threshold Tuning: Deployed AI data-matching tools to programmatically identify, standardise, and merge malformed records across systems. Continually fine-tuned AI confidence thresholds to mitigate initial false-positive matches, instituting a human-in-the-loop review for complex edge cases.
  • Complex Deduplication Logic: Engineered advanced merging rules to navigate highly complex database conflicts, specifically addressing the identification of Partner versus End User records scattered across duplicate account types.
  • Cross-Functional Governance: Brokered clear, enterprise-wide rules of engagement for record ownership and merging permissions, successfully navigating departmental resistance when standardising highly guarded records like Support Contacts.
  • Automated Hygiene Cadence: Transitioned the business from ad-hoc, manual list scrubbing to a highly governed, automated monthly data hygiene cadence.
Result
Processed over 2,000,000 records, successfully reducing database duplicates by 25% while achieving a 90% deduplication accuracy rate. The AI-driven standardisation boosted email segmentation accuracy to over 80%, drastically reduced email bounces, improved lead scoring precision, and slashed manual data preparation time by 50%. The automated monthly cadence remains the baseline data hygiene standard for the business.
Reflection
This project demonstrated that managing an enterprise database at scale requires more than just basic deduplication rules; it requires advanced, AI-assisted pattern recognition. Navigating AI false-positives and complex account hierarchies reinforced that successful automation must always be paired with rigorous data governance and continuous cross-departmental alignment on data ownership.
Project 2

Funnel Optimisation & Lead Prioritisation Architecture · Company 1

Situation
The organisation lacked a formal operational bridge between marketing campaign execution and BDR outreach execution. Without a unified prioritisation framework, high-intent marketing qualified leads (MQLs) were subjected to standard, unoptimised outreach queues, leading to delayed speed-to-lead times, inconsistent follow-up cadences, and lost pipeline velocity.
Task
My objective was to architect a cross-functional lead prioritisation and SLA framework that mapped specific marketing campaign types and engagement behaviours directly to tiered BDR outreach sequences. I needed to negotiate and establish strict lead-handoff SLAs, design a continuous feedback loop for campaign optimisation, and broker alignment between marketing and sales development leadership on tier definitions.
Action
  • Tiered Prioritisation Architecture: Engineered a tiered lead matrix categorizing inbound activity into clear priority levels (P1, P2, P3) based on campaign type and member status behaviour, ensuring high-intent leads received immediate visibility.
  • Sequence & Timeline Mapping: Aligned the prioritisation matrix directly with specific BDR outreach sequences, dictating strict, automated rules for the exact order and required timeframes for rep contact.
  • Cross-Functional Matrix Negotiation: Managed intensive alignment sessions between Marketing and BDR stakeholders to resolve conflicting priorities and secure a unified consensus on which campaign values mapped to each priority tier.
  • Funnel Governance & Documentation: Authored comprehensive global lead handoff documentation and engineered a feedback mechanism, allowing BDRs to signal lead quality back to marketing for campaign optimisation.
  • Iterative Framework Scaling: Managed the continuous optimisation of the framework through multiple deployment phases, scaling the model through to a highly refined Version 3 (V3) deployment.
Result
Successfully delivered a high-velocity inbound funnel framework that substantially reduced lead response times for P1 leads and accelerated pipeline progression from MQL to Sales Qualified Lead (SQL). The standardised V3 framework resulted in a significantly higher proportion of sales-ready opportunities and established a transparent, accountable SLA culture between marketing and the sales development organisation.
Reflection
This project illustrated that lead generation is only as good as lead velocity. Building a successful prioritisation framework requires looking beyond system integrations to define the precise human behaviours and timeframes needed upon lead arrival, proving that operational success is achieved by continually iterating on cross-functional alignment to keep pace with evolving GTM execution.

Situation

Task
My objective was to design, scale, and govern an automated marketing attribution architecture across progressive phases. The system needed to move the business from manual reporting to an automated, multi-touch account-level framework, eliminate the highly restrictive data-entry requirements of the initial CRM-native model, consolidate complex pipeline data into a single PowerBI source of truth, and navigate intense stakeholder negotiations regarding attribution timeframes.
Action
  • Phase 1 Framework (Contact-Based Last-Touch): Engineered an initial Last-Touch attribution model utilising Salesforce's Campaign Influence module, which required strict manual matching of specific MQL names and timestamps on contact records tied to opportunities, creating a heavy manual data-auditing bottleneck.
  • Phase 2 Evolution (Account-Based MTA): Partnered with the Performance Analytics team to transition the entire framework into PowerBI. Re-engineered the backend logic to track holistic account-level engagement, completely removing the restrictive Phase 1 contact data-dependency and eliminating hours of manual data preparation.
  • Data Modeling & Stakeholder Alignment: Developed complex data-modeling logic in PowerBI to account for diverse customer types. Managed lengthy, intensive debates with cross-functional stakeholders to align on standardised attribution lookback timeframes, managing pushback from teams advocating for longer campaign-influence windows.
  • Dashboard Consolidation: Built unified, real-time performance dashboards within PowerBI, consolidating global pipeline data, regional campaigns, and customer segmentation into a centralised repository.
Result
Successfully established an executive-level single source of truth for marketing performance, reducing reporting latency from 5 days to near-real-time while severely curtailing manual operational overhead. Shifting to the Phase 2 account-based model increased recognised marketing attribution by 30% and provided a standardised framework for cross-regional campaign ROI, despite ongoing stakeholder alignment challenges regarding lookback durations.
Reflection
This project demonstrated that scaling an analytics function requires removing data-entry dependencies from front-line users. Moving the attribution logic out of manual CRM fields and into automated PowerBI data schemas proved that operations must focus on account-level collectives rather than siloed contact points. Furthermore, managing the lookback window debates highlighted that defining attribution timelines is an ongoing exercise in stakeholder compromise.

Result
Successfully established an immediate, auditable data privacy baseline while driving global alignment on regional compliance variances. Surfacing Marketo's captured data source fields directly inside Salesforce closed a critical visibility gap for front-line teams, while the Jira framework introduced a rigorous audit trail for compliance tracking, significantly lowering corporate legal exposure.

This project highlighted that global operations leaders must frequently bridge geographic and cultural gaps in regulatory understanding. When a US-centric corporate framework clashes with local international compliance laws, an operations leader must act as a strategic educator—finding clever platform configuration workarounds to protect the business while continuously lobbying for long-term global data governance standards.

Cross-Functional Data Governance & Support Contact Integration Initiative · Company 1

Situation
The CRM architecture contained a critical blind spot: records designated as "Authorised Support Contacts" were entirely locked down by the Support team to prevent external tools from disrupting system access. Consequently, Marketo was blocked from updating these records, preventing marketing from tracking attribution, appending intent signals, or—most critically—enforcing global opt-out and privacy compliance requests.
Task
My objective was to broker an integration framework that would surface marketing engagement and compliance data on support records within Salesforce without compromising their support centre access. I needed to design a secure technical compromise to unlock sales visibility and attribution reporting, and ultimately escalate the deadlock to executive leadership when revenue and regulatory compliance were at risk.
Action
  • Risk & Revenue Impact Analysis: Quantified the strategic impact of the data silo, demonstrating how the block obscured account-level marketing attribution, starved sales of intent signals, and created severe legal compliance risks regarding opt-out enforcement.
  • Technical Compromise Architecture: Proposed a highly governed workaround that restricted Marketo's write permissions exclusively to non-system fields (such as attribution, intent signals, and opt-out checkboxes), guaranteeing zero impact on core support functionalities.
  • Cross-Functional Negotiation: Partnered with the Support team to address their security concerns, acting as the operational mediator between marketing's data needs and support's operational guardrails.
  • Executive Escalation & Governance: When the technical compromise was rejected at the team level, I prepared the business case and escalated the impasse to the respective Department Heads to resolve the structural data ownership conflict.
Result
Successfully elevated a critical operational bottleneck to executive-level visibility. While the escalation forced strategic conversations between Department Heads regarding data governance, the Support team maintained their strict system lockdown; as a result, the current state requires these vital records to be manually updated one by one. By fully documenting the business case, I established the baseline data and compliance risks to justify future automated remediation.
Reflection
This project illustrated that operations is frequently about navigating complex corporate data ownership boundaries. When a technical impasse forces a team into a highly inefficient, manual "one-by-one" update process, an operations leader's role is to exhaust all technical compromises, protect compliance as much as possible, and leave a fully audited business case ready for the next phase of enterprise infrastructure maturity.
